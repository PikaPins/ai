### [1] [Backdoor Attacks on Decentralised Post-Training](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02372)
*Oğuzhan Ersoy,Nikolay Blagoev,Jona te Lintelo,Stefanos Koffas,Marina Krček,Stjepan Picek*

Main category: cs.CR

TL;DR: 该论文首次研究了管道并行性中的后训练后门攻击，攻击者通过控制管道中间阶段而非整个模型或数据集，导致模型训练失准。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 随着大型语言模型的发展，后训练时的数据和管道并行性技术被广泛使用，但这种技术存在被恶意参与者利用进行攻击的风险。当前研究主要集中在数据并行性和联邦学习的攻击与防御，管道并行性的研究相对较少，特别是在对抗后门攻击方面。因此，为了增强管道并行性的安全性，首次提出了针对管道并行性的后门攻击。

Method: 该方法通过控制管道模型的中间阶段来实施后门攻击。实验中使用特定的触发词来引导模型在后期的训练中偏离正常路径，以此评估攻击的效果。

Result: 实验结果表明，即使攻击者具有有限的控制，也能成功注入后门攻击，导致模型训练失准。在触发词的影响下，模型的准确性从80%降至6%。即使后续进行了安全对齐训练，后门攻击依然有60%的成功率。

Conclusion: 该研究提高了管道并行性在大型语言模型后训练中的可信度，为防御这类攻击提供了理论依据和技术路径。

Abstract: Decentralised post-training of large language models utilises data and pipeline parallelism techniques to split the data and the model. Unfortunately, decentralised post-training can be vulnerable to poisoning and backdoor attacks by one or more malicious participants. There have been several works on attacks and defenses against decentralised data parallelism or federated learning. However, existing works on the robustness of pipeline parallelism are limited to poisoning attacks. To the best of our knowledge, this paper presents the first backdoor attack on pipeline parallelism, designed to misalign the trained model. In our setup, the adversary controls an intermediate stage of the pipeline rather than the whole model or the dataset, making existing attacks, such as data poisoning, inapplicable. Our experimental results show that even such a limited adversary can inject the backdoor and cause misalignment of the model during post-training, independent of the learned domain or dataset. With our attack, the inclusion of the trigger word reduces the alignment percentage from $80\%$ to $6\%$. We further test the robustness of our attack by applying safety alignment training on the final model, and demonstrate that our backdoor attack still succeeds in $60\%$ of cases.

Existing machine learning and deep learning approaches typically depend on labeled datasets, handcrafted features, supervised training, or dynamic analysis, which limits their scalability and effectiveness in open-world scenarios.

### [3] [Opal: Private Memory for Personal AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02522)
*Darya Kaviani,Alp Eren Ozdarendeli,Jinhao Zhu,Yu Ding,Raluca Ada Popa*

Main category: cs.CR

TL;DR: Opal是一款私密记忆系统，通过将依赖数据的推理集中在可信封密环境中，并使用透明存取模式与轻量级的知识图谱来处理持续的数据摄取，从而在保护隐私的同时提高了检索准确性和吞吐量。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>

Method: Opal的设计采用了分割依赖数据推理的策略，将这一组件置于可信区域内，并且使用了透明存取模式来减少系统对外部存储系统的依赖，此外，系统还通过轻量级知识图谱处理持续数据摄取，优化检索准确性。

Result: 实验展示了Opal相较于仅使用语义检索的系统在检索准确性上有13个百分点的提升，在吞吐量上达到了29倍的提高，基础设施成本降低了15倍。

Conclusion: Opal考虑在未来部署于大型AI供应商的百万级用户群体中，展示了其在实际应用场景中的应用潜力。

Abstract: Personal AI systems increasingly retain long-term memory of user activity, including documents, emails, messages, meetings, and ambient recordings. Trusted hardware can keep this data private, but struggles to scale with a growing datastore. This pushes the data to external storage, which exposes retrieval access patterns that leak private information to the application provider. Oblivious RAM (ORAM) is a cryptographic primitive that can hide these patterns, but it requires a fixed access budget, precluding the query-dependent traversals that agentic memory systems rely on for accuracy.
  We present Opal, a private memory system for personal AI. Our key insight is to decouple all data-dependent reasoning from the bulk of personal data, confining it to the trusted enclave. Untrusted disk then sees only fixed, oblivious memory accesses. This enclave-resident component uses a lightweight knowledge graph to capture personal context that semantic search alone misses and handles continuous ingestion by piggybacking reindexing and capacity management on every ORAM access. Evaluated on a comprehensive synthetic personal-data pipeline driven by stochastic communication models, Opal improves retrieval accuracy by 13 percentage points over semantic search and achieves 29x higher throughput with 15x lower infrastructure cost than a secure baseline. Opal is under consideration for deployment to millions of users at a major AI provider.

### [7] [Analyzing Healthcare Interoperability Vulnerabilities: Formal Modeling and Graph-Theoretic Approach](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03043)
