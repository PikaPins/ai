### [1] [Hermes Seal: Zero-Knowledge Assurance for Autonomous Vehicle Communications](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.26343)
*Munawar Hasan,Apostol Vassilev,Edward Griffor,Thoshitha Gamage*

Main category: cs.CR

TL;DR: 本研究提出了基于零知识证明（ZKPs）的Hermes Seal框架，用于实现车辆之间的隐私保护、可验证通信。通过gpu实现实时证明生成和验证，验证了在verifiable自主系统信息交换方面的实际应用。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 随着自动驾驶系统在监管合规、透明审计和分布式环境中的信任运行方面的需求增长，提出了Hermes Seal框架来解决敏感或专有数据的安全问题。

Method: 本研究采用了基于zk-SNARK的零知识证明方法，设计了一套隐私保护、验证通信的框架，应用于车辆之间的通信场景。

Result: 通过Hermes Seal框架，完成了两例实际案例的研究，并进行了实际性能测试，证明了框架的有效性。实时证明生成和验证可在GPU上实现，具体指标分别为8毫秒生成和1毫秒验证。

Conclusion: Hermes Seal框架不仅支持跨系统间的信息互操作性，还通过零知识证明保护了系统内部数据的隐私性，为可验证的自主系统信息交换提供了技术方案。

Abstract: The application of zero-knowledge proofs (ZKPs) in autonomous systems is an emerging area of research, motivated by the growing need for regulatory compliance, transparent auditing, and trustworthy operation in decentralized environments. zk-SNARK is a powerful cryptographic tool that allows a party (the prover) to prove to another party (the verifier) that a statement about its own internal state is true, without revealing sensitive or proprietary data about that state. This paper proposes Hermes Seal: a zk-SNARK-based ZKP framework for enabling privacy-preserving, verifiable communication in vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V) and vehicle-to-infrastructure (V2I) networks. The framework allows autonomous systems to generate cryptographic proofs of perception and decision-related computations without revealing proprietary models, sensor data, or internal system states, thereby supporting interoperability across heterogeneous autonomous systems. We present two real-world case studies implemented and empirically evaluated within our framework, demonstrating a step toward verifiable autonomous system information exchanges. The first demonstrates real-time proof generation and verification, achieving 8 ms proof generation and 1 ms verification on a GPU, while the second evaluates the performance of an autonomous vehicle perception stack, enabling proof of computation without exposing proprietary or confidential data. Furthermore, the framework can be integrated into AV perception stacks to facilitate verifiable interoperability and privacy-preserving cooperative perception. The demonstration code for this project is open source, available on Github.

### [2] [Hidden Elo: Private Matchmaking through Encrypted Rating Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.26407)
*Mindaugas Budzys,Bin Liu,Antonis Michalas*

Main category: cs.CR

TL;DR: H-Elo是一个基于全同态加密（FHE）的私密评分系统，它允许通过传统评分系统进行安全匹配，同时保持评分值的私密性和安全性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统的匹配算法需要收集敏感/个人信息才能有效运行，这会导致隐私泄露。本文旨在减少匹配应用中的隐私泄露。

Method: 本文提出了H-Elo，一种基于全同态加密（FHE）的私密评分系统，用于实现通过传统评分系统进行的安全匹配。

Result: 通过实验，H-Elo能实现类似明文实现的准确度，同时保护评分值的私密性和安全性。此外，还对比了H-Elo与其他私密匹配解决方案。

Conclusion: H-Elo是目前为止第一个基于私密评分系统且安全的匹配协议，为该领域的发展提供了新的方向。

Abstract: Matchmaking has become a prevalent part in contemporary applications, being used in dating apps, social media, online games, contact tracing and in various other use-cases. However, most implementations of matchmaking require the collection of sensitive/personal data for proper functionality. As such, with this work we aim to reduce the privacy leakage inherent in matchmaking applications. We propose H-Elo, a Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE)-based, private rating system, which allows for secure matchmaking through the use of traditional rating systems. In this work, we provide the construction of H-Elo, analyse the security of it against a capable adversary as well as benchmark our construction in a chess-based rating update scenario. Through our experiments we show that H-Elo can achieve similar accuracy to a plaintext implementation, while keeping rating values private and secure. Additionally, we compare our work to other private matchmaking solutions as well as cover some future directions in the field of private matchmaking. To the best of our knowledge we provide one of the first private and secure rating system-based matchmaking protocols.

### [3] [Towards Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning using Hybrid Homomorphic Encryption](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.26417)
*Ivan Costa,Pedro Correia,Ivone Amorim,Eva Maia,Isabel Praça*

Main category: cs.CR

TL;DR: 该研究通过将两种不同的密钥保护机制融入HHE-FL流程，提高了联邦学习中模型的隐私安全性。研究利用花束框架在PASTA/BFV同态加密方案上实现了这两种机制，并在MNIST数据集上评估了它们。结果表明，这两种机制在保持模型准确性的同时，增加了少量的开销。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有的HHE-FL系统依赖于所有客户端共享的单一同态密钥对，这使得如果客户端行为不当或劫持其他客户端的流量，敏感更新信息容易被捕获。因此，研究旨在提高HHE-FL系统的安全性。

Method: 研究将两种密钥保护机制（遮蔽和RSA封装）整合到HHE-FL流程中，其中遮蔽机制是在同态加密前对客户端密钥进行匿名处理，随后由服务器异步解匿名。RSA封装是将同态加密的密钥额外包装在服务器的RSA公钥下。研究使用花束框架在PASTA/BFV同态加密方案上实现了这两种机制，并在MNIST数据集上进行评估。

Result: 研究发现，这两种机制在保持模型准确性的同时，增加了适量的开销。遮蔽机制几乎不影响性能，而RSA封装方法仅带来适度的运行时间和通信开销。

Conclusion: 研究证明了通过使用密钥保护机制，即使在具有恶意参与者的对抗性环境中，HHE-FL可以保持模型的准确性和安全性。

Abstract: Federated Learning (FL) enables collaborative training while keeping sensitive data on clients' devices, but local model updates can still leak private information. Hybrid Homomorphic Encryption (HHE) has recently been applied to FL to mitigate client overhead while preserving privacy. However, existing HHE-FL systems rely on a single homomorphic key pair shared across all clients, which forces them to assume an unrealistically weak threat model: if a client misbehaves or intercepts another's traffic, private updates can be exposed. We eliminate this weakness by integrating two alternative key protection mechanisms into the HHE-FL workflow. The first is masking, where client keys are blinded before homomorphic encryption and later unblinded homomorphically by the server. The second is RSA encapsulation, where homomorphically encrypted keys are additionally wrapped under the server's RSA public key. These countermeasures prevent key misuse by other clients and extend HHE-FL security to adversarial settings with malicious participants. We implement both approaches on top of the Flower framework using the PASTA/BFV HHE scheme and evaluate them on the MNIST dataset with 12 clients. Results show that both mechanisms preserve model accuracy while adding minimal overhead: masking incurs negligible cost, and RSA encapsulation introduces only modest runtime and communication overhead.
